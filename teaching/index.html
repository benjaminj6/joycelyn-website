About the Studio

At it’s core, my studio is about love and community. In my studio, I like to think of my students not as violinists, but as just regular kids who play the violin well. If you’re looking for a violin teacher who is not only concerned about her student’s technique, but is also genuinely interested in the lives of her students, then you’ve come to the right place.

Suzuki Method

"Music exists for the purpose of growing an admirable heart” - Dr. Shinichi Suzuki

From personal experience, I can say that the Suzuki method made me into a musician. Don’t get me wrong, I’m not referring to someone who can play an instrument or sing and read notes. I’m referring to the person who has felt their heart and soul fill with warmth upon hearing a glorious and beautiful piece and will stop at nothing to feel and share that warmth again and again and again. As a Suzuki teacher, my goal for my students is musicianship, not task completion. If you are a prospective student, please read briefly about the how the Suzuki Method helps students work toward musicianship.
